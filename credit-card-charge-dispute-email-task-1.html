Task 1

You have been using the same credit card for the past 10 years with no issues. On your last bill, you noticed an unfamiliar $50 charge. You did not recognize this transaction and are certain that this is a mistake. Write an email to the credit card company in about 150-200 words.

Your email should do the following things: -

  • Describe the problem with your credit card statement.
  • Explain why you are certain that the charge is a mistake.
  • Explain how you would like the problem to be solved.
Dear Mastercard Team,

I am writing regarding my credit card ending with 4321’s recent statement.

On my last statement dated 2025-10-06, I was mistakenly charged for a meal at YYZ airport, Toronto. The transaction number is #Food1234 and date is 2025-09-15. The dollar amount is $50.00 and restaurant name is Tim Hortons.

I have been using Mastercard’s services for a decade, and I have never seen any unauthorised transaction in my account. However, on the day of transaction, I was in India for my brother’s marriage and I did call the customer service about my departure. Therefore, there is no chance of me having lunch at this restaurant. You can check my previous transaction where I booked a flight ticket to India via Air Canada airline.

Since I was out of the country and this is an unauthorised transaction, hence, I would like you to remove this transaction from my statement and refund the $50. Moreover, I suggest, sending a one-time password to customer’ phone to validate transaction to prevent scams.

Thank you for your attention to this matter. I look forward to hearing a prompt solution.

Sincerely,
Mark Thomas
